1.0 Purpose and Benefits

Appropriate organizational use of information and information technology (“IT”) resources and effective security of those resources require the participation and support of {{entity_name}}’s workforce (“users”). Inappropriate use exposes the organization to potential risks including virus attacks, compromise of network systems and services, and legal issues.

2.0 Authority

{{entity_name}}

3.0 Scope

This policy applies to users of any system’s information or physical infrastructure regardless of its form or format created or used to support {{entity_name}}. It is the user’s responsibility to read and understand this policy and to conduct their activities in accordance with its terms. In addition, users must read and understand the organization’s Information Security Policy and its associated standards.

4.0 Information Statement

Users should have no legitimate expectation of privacy during any use of {{entity_name}}'s IT resources. Any use may be monitored, intercepted, recorded, read, copied, accessed, or captured in any manner by authorized personnel without additional prior notice to individuals. Periodic monitoring will be conducted for all IT systems and electronic communications.

The organization may impose restrictions at the discretion of {{CIO_equivalent_title}} on the use of specific IT resources, such as blocking access to certain websites or services not serving legitimate business purposes.

4.1 Acceptable Use

All uses of IT resources must comply with {{entity_name}}'s policies, standards, procedures, and guidelines, as well as applicable laws. Users are responsible for protecting the confidentiality, integrity, and availability of {{entity_name}}'s information.

4.2 Unacceptable Use

Unacceptable use includes, but is not limited to, unauthorized access or disclosure of sensitive information, installing unapproved software, connecting unauthorized devices to the network, and using IT resources for personal commercial purposes without approval from {{CIO_equivalent_title}}.

4.3 Occasional and Incidental Personal Use

Occasional personal use of IT resources is permitted, provided it does not interfere with work responsibilities or violate {{entity_name}}'s policies. {{entity_name}} may revoke or limit this privilege at any time.

4.4 Individual Accountability

Users are accountable for the activities performed under their User ID. Unauthorized access or sharing of credentials is strictly prohibited and may result in termination.

4.5 Restrictions on Off-Site Transmission and Storage of Information

Transmission or storage of {{entity_name}}'s restricted, sensitive, or confidential information must comply with all {{entity_name}}'s security policies.

4.6 Mobile Devices and BYOD

Users accessing {{entity_name}}'s IT resources via personal devices must comply with {{entity_name}}'s Mobile Device Management (MDM) requirements.

4.7 User Responsibility for IT Equipment

Users are responsible for securing all IT equipment assigned to them. In case of loss, theft, or damage, users must provide a written report to {{responsible_department_name}}.

4.8 Clean Desk and Clear Screen

Users are required to secure all sensitive information at the end of the workday and during absences. Screens must be locked when leaving devices unattended.

5.0 Compliance

This policy shall take effect upon publication. Compliance with {{entity_name}}'s policies is mandatory. Non-compliance may result in penalties, including termination.
